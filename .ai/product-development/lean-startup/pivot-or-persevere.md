# Pivot or Persevere

A pivot is a structured course correction designed to test a new fundamental hypothesis. It is not a failure — it is what turns failed experiments into usable knowledge. The hardest part is not deciding how to pivot; it is admitting that you should.

---

## The Decision

Every experiment ends with one of three outcomes. Commit to the decision before looking at the data.

### DO: Force a pivot-or-persevere meeting after every experiment cycle

After each experiment, the team reviews results against pre-set targets and decides:

| Outcome | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Persevere** | Hit or exceeded target metric | Double down, run next experiment on the growth hypothesis |
| **Pivot** | Missed target but learned something exploitable | Change one fundamental assumption, re-run |
| **Kill** | No signal at all, or third consecutive miss on the same hypothesis | Stop. Redirect appetite to a different bet |

### DON'T: Let "almost there" delay the decision

"We're close, just one more iteration" is the most expensive sentence in product development. If the metric didn't hit the target, it didn't hit the target. Either the hypothesis is wrong (pivot) or the execution is wrong (fix and re-run once). Two misses on the same hypothesis with different execution = pivot.

---

## Pivot Types

Not all pivots are the same. Name the type so the team understands what's changing and what's preserved.

### DO: Pick the pivot that preserves the most validated learning

| Pivot type | What changes | What stays | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Zoom-in** | A single feature becomes the whole product | The feature that users actually use | Project tool → just the chat feature |
| **Zoom-out** | Current product becomes a feature of something larger | The core value, now embedded in a bigger offering | Chat widget → full customer support platform |
| **Customer segment** | Who you serve | The product | Built for freelancers → sells to agencies |
| **Customer need** | Which problem you solve | The customer relationship | Task tracking → time tracking for the same users |
| **Channel** | How you reach customers | Product and segment | Self-serve signup → sales-led |
| **Revenue model** | How you charge | Product and segment | Subscription → usage-based pricing |
| **Technology** | How you build it | The problem and customer | Native app → web-based (same value, cheaper delivery) |
| **Platform** | Single app → platform (or reverse) | Core value proposition | Single tool → marketplace |

### DON'T: Pivot everything at once

A pivot changes one fundamental hypothesis. If you change the customer, the problem, and the solution simultaneously, you're not pivoting — you're starting over. Starting over is fine, but call it what it is.

## Recognizing the Signal

### DO: Watch for these pivot triggers

- **Flat cohort curves**: New cohorts don't perform better than old ones despite product changes
- **One feature carries all engagement**: Users ignore 80% of the product — zoom-in pivot candidate
- **Wrong customer shows up**: The people paying aren't who you designed for — customer segment pivot
- **"Love the product, won't pay"**: Value exists but revenue model is wrong
- **Acquisition works, retention doesn't**: The problem is real, but your solution doesn't solve it well enough

### DON'T: Confuse a bad execution with a bad hypothesis

Before pivoting, verify:

1. Did enough people see the experiment? (sample size)
2. Did the experiment actually test the hypothesis? (experiment validity)
3. Was the experience broken? (execution quality)

If execution was poor, fix and re-test. Pivot only when a well-executed experiment misses the target.

---

## The Pivot Meeting

### DO: Schedule it in advance, not in response to bad news

Put a recurring pivot-or-persevere review on the calendar — every 2 weeks or at the end of each cycle. This removes the emotional charge. The meeting happens whether results are good or bad.

Agenda:
1. **Read the data** — metric vs target, no narrative
2. **Name the outcome** — persevere, pivot (which type), or kill
3. **If pivot: define the new hypothesis** — using the experiment template from the [manifesto](lean-startup-manifesto.md)
4. **Commit** — new experiment starts within 48 hours

### DON'T: Let the pivot meeting become a brainstorming session

The meeting decides one thing: does the current hypothesis hold? If not, the next hypothesis should come from what you learned, not from a whiteboard session of new ideas. Creativity happens during shaping, not during pivot reviews.
